The slow spine of the archive lives here. These are the longer pieces: essays that begin from a painting, a novel, a city, a private humiliation, or some political absurdity and then keep following the thought until it becomes uncomfortable.

I do not think of them as reviews or explainers. They are attempts to make literature, psychoanalysis, history, and biography touch the same nerve. If there is a method, it is this: start from an image or a wound, and only then ask theory to speak.

If you are new to the blog, these are good places to begin:

- [The Stranger at the Feast](/posts/the-stranger-at-the-feast)
- [The Grand Inquisitor in the Age of Algorithms](/posts/the-grand-inquisitor-in-the-age-of-algorithms)

- [What Is Marxism and Why It Still Matters](/posts/what-is-marxism-and-why-it-matters)

These are the pieces in which the blog becomes most fully itself. For shorter image-centered entries, go to [Studies](/studies). For the recurrent ideas that bind everything together, go to [Motifs](/motifs).
