Every archive develops habits before its author notices them. It clusters around certain years, returns compulsively to certain moods, grows long when a subject matters, and becomes brief when it is only passing through. This page is a way of looking at the blog from a slight distance.

The charts below are not the truth of the archive. They cannot measure force, only rhythm. Still, they reveal something useful: when the writing arrived in bursts, when it slowed, and how often a piece demanded more space than a quick note could give it.

## Posts over time

This chart shows the visible rhythm of publication: periods of compression, return, and silence. On this blog, silence is not always absence. Often it means a piece is taking longer than expected, or that an idea has not yet found its form.

## Reading time distribution

This chart shows something closer to temperament. I rarely trust a thought that resolves too quickly, which is why the archive keeps drifting toward longer essays. Even the shorter pieces tend to circle the subject before they leave it.
