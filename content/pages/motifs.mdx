---
title: Motifs
description: The recurring obsessions that keep returning across the archive.
---

This blog repeats itself on purpose. Certain images return until they become a private vocabulary. What follows is not a system, only a map of the motifs that keep insisting.

## Melancholy and loss

Here the archive circles grief, exile, unnamed sadness, and the difficulty of releasing what was never fully possessed. Start with [The Demon and the Melancholic](/posts/the-demon-and-the-melancholic), [The Demon Seated](/paintings/vrubel-demon-1890), and [Adele Bloch-Bauer I](/paintings/gustav-klimt-adele-bloch-bauer-I).

## Strangerhood and language

These pieces ask what it means to be seen before one has spoken, to carry a name, a language, or a body that enters the room before the self does. Start with [The Stranger at the Feast](/posts/the-stranger-at-the-feast), [City](/posts/city-a-poem-from-vladimir-kotlyarov), and [Stańczyk and the Paradox of the Sad Clown](/posts/stanczky-and-the-paradox-of-the-sad-clown).

## Obsession and repetition

This is the part of the archive devoted to gambling, scrolling, jealousy, compulsion, and the strange enjoyment of self-damage. Start with [The Gambler and the Black Square](/posts/the-gambler-and-the-black-square), [The Garden of Earthly Delights and the Underground Man](/posts/the-garden-of-earthly-delights-and-the-underground-man), and [Jealousy](/paintings/edvard-munch-jealousy-1895).

## Politics, class, and spectacle

Here the archive turns outward without ceasing to be psychological. Class struggle, public art, democratic theater, and ideological enjoyment meet in the same field. Start with [What Is Marxism and Why It Still Matters](/posts/what-is-marxism-and-why-it-matters), [The Grand Inquisitor in the Age of Algorithms](/posts/the-grand-inquisitor-in-the-age-of-algorithms), and [Man, Controller of the Universe and the Art of Resistance](/paintings/diego-rivera-man-at-the-crossroads-1934).

The motifs overlap because the archive does. Melancholy becomes politics, politics becomes desire, desire becomes language, and language eventually returns as an image.
